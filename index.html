Lead Instructor

Er. Anzar Hussain Lone

Assistant Professor, Dept of CSE, LPU

Anzar Hussain Lone is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at Lovely Professional University, Punjab. With a strong academic background, he holds a B.Tech and M.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering, having completed his master’s degree from National Institute of Technology (NIT) Srinagar. He is also a two-time GATE qualifier, demonstrating his deep understanding of core computer science concepts.

With over three years of teaching experience, he has been actively involved in academic instruction, research, and mentoring. His primary research interests include Artificial Intelligence, Data Science, and Machine Learning applications. Mr. Lone has authored four research papers in the domain of Artificial Intelligence, and is currently guiding 2 masters students and 15 B.Tech students on research and innovation projects.

An ardent researcher and educator, he has conducted several workshops and training programs on Artificial Intelligence and Prompt Engineering for students and educators. His academic and professional contributions reflect his commitment to fostering innovation, critical thinking, and applied research in emerging technologies.

Course Structure

Course Label SDC

Mode & Lang Online / Eng
Level Advanced
Duration 60 Hrs
01

Week 1: Introduction of AI and Fundamentals Of Python

Content Delivery Duration: 10 Hours

  • Day 1 Introduction to Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning Applications across domains (business, healthcare, automation, vision, language), Overview of the ML pipeline, types of AI (narrow, general), Key AI problems and techniques, Modern AI Toolkits (TensorFlow, PyTorch)
  • Day 2 Python basics: Variables, data types, operators, Control flow in Python, Conditional statements, Loops, Variables, data types, operators and practice implementation.
  • Day 3 Python data structures: Lists, tuples, sets, dictionaries
  • Day 4 Functions, Modules and packages, File handling
  • Day 5 Introduction to NumPy, Arrays and operations, Mathematical operations with NumPy
02

Week 2: Python for Data Science

Content Delivery Duration: 10 Hours

  • Day 6 Introduction to Pandas, DataFrames and Series, Practical implementation
  • Day 7 EDA: Data visualization using Matplotlib & Seaborn
  • Day 8 Types of data, Data pre-processing, data leakage concept, handling missing values, outliers handling, handling categorical data, scaling and normalization, class imbalance handling
  • Day 9 Feature Engineering: variance threshold Feature Selection, correlation-based removal Feature Selection, forward selection, backward elimination, tree-based feature importance
  • Day 10 Feature Extraction, creating new features, aggregation features, Dimensionality Reduction: Curse of dimensionality, Principal Component Analysis, explained variance ration, Linear Discriminant Analysis
03

Week 3: Machine Learning Algorithms

Content Delivery Duration: 10 Hours

  • Day 11 Introduction to Machine Learning algorithms, Linear Regression with practical implementation
  • Day 12 Logistic Regression: Classification problems (Maths behind Logistic algorithm and practical implementation)
  • Day 13 Types of Linear Regression and Regularization, Different types of Gradient Decent
  • Day 14 K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), Decision Tree Algorithm and Random Forest
  • Day 15 Continuous Assessment (CA) 1
04

Week 4: Evaluation Matrix and Ensemble Learning

Content Delivery Duration: 10 Hours

  • Day 16 Support Vector Machine and Model evaluation: Confusion Matrix, Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1 Score, ROC Curve, Precision-Recall Curve, Cross Validation
  • Day 17 Ensemble Learning: Introduction to ensemble learning, Bagging & Boosting Ensembles
  • Day 18 Cross Validation and Hyperparameter tuning
  • Day 19 Project-1
  • Day 20 Project-2
05

Week 5: Unsupervised Learning

Content Delivery Duration: 10 Hours

  • Day 21 Unsupervised Learning: Role of unsupervised learning, Differences between supervised and unsupervised learning, Euclidean, Manhattan, Cosine distances, Choosing appropriate distance metrics
  • Day 22 K-Means Clustering, Elbow method, K-Medoids, Hierarchical Clustering
  • Day 23 Introduction to Deep Learning, Neural Networks, Neurons and layers
  • Day 24 Activation functions, ReLU, Sigmoid, Tanh, Loss functions and optimizers, Gradient Descent, SGD, Adam
  • Day 25 ANN and Practical Implementation with different Deep learning tools
06

Week 6: CNN and RNN

Content Delivery Duration: 10 Hours

  • Day 26 CNN: Filters (kernels), Stride and padding, Activation functions (ReLU), Pooling layers (Max Pooling, Average Pooling), Flatten layer
  • Day 27 RNN: types, Sequential data and time-series data, Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM), Gated Recurrent Units (GRU)
  • Day 28 Project-3
  • Day 29 Continuous Assessment (CA) 2
  • Day 30 Project-4
4 Hands-on Projects
